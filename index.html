01 / about

Born to build.

I grew up in Sircilla, a small town in Telangana. As a kid I was obsessed with building—blocks became bridges, bridges became miniature cities. That instinct to construct never left.

I've been building systems since childhood—today, I build them with code, data, and AI.

  1. chapter 01

    Childhood in Sircilla

    Wooden blocks, LEGO, cardboard skylines. I would tear apart remote controls just to see the circuitry. Curiosity was the default setting.

  2. chapter 02

    Pencil art & chess

    Spent years on graphite portraits and still-life studies. Competed in district-level chess tournaments—podium finishes taught me patience and pattern recognition.

  3. chapter 03

    The radium shop years

    Worked part-time at a local radium/flex printing shop. Designed logos, flex banners, visiting cards in CorelDRAW and Photoshop. Learned typography, kerning, and what clients actually want.

  4. chapter 04

    Transition to software

    Picked up Python during college, fell hard for data structures, then ML. Design eye + engineering brain turned out to be a rare pairing.

  5. chapter 05

    What I build now

    Full systems from scratch—auth, APIs, ML pipelines, clean UIs. The goal is a startup of my own: a product that solves a real, stubborn problem.

05 / problem solving

Problem → Action → Outcome.

Real issues I've hit in production and the way I worked through them.

Authentication
problem

Users stayed logged in even after password changes.

action

Rebuilt auth with short-lived JWTs + refresh tokens, added token rotation and a server-side blacklist.

outcome

Zero stale-session incidents in 6 months.

Reliability
problem

External APIs failed silently, crashing downstream jobs.

action

Introduced typed error envelopes, retries with exponential backoff, and structured logging.

outcome

Error visibility up 10x; recovery automated for 80% of transient failures.

ML Ops
problem

ML model accuracy dropped in production vs. notebook.

action

Built a preprocessing library shared by training and serving; pinned schema via Pydantic.

outcome

Closed a 9-point accuracy gap; eliminated training-serving skew.

System Design
problem

Monolith was slow to deploy and scary to change.

action

Carved out auth and reporting into services, standardized on gateway + queue pattern.

outcome

Deploy time 18m → 4m; incident blast radius shrunk.
